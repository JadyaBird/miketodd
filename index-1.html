A FEW words about us

All About How Awesome I Am.

For many little boys, being a race car driver tops the list of ‘most awesome job ever’. This too was on Mike Todd’s list. But it was music that won out. Really, there was never even a choice. “Music chose me”, Mike says.

Mike’s journey in music started before he can even remember. “There’s a story my parents tell of me as a two year old standing on top of my grandparents kitchen table singing and stomping my feet along to the radio,” he says. Mike comes from a long line of Todd musicians, including both his father and uncle. Growing up with these touring musicians in the family certainly proved to be an early influence in his life.

Like many young musicians Mike gravitated toward guitar driven music and spent much time lifting the searing melodies that would scream from the stereo at all times of the day. School was just something he did in between exploring music (and girls). But his big leap in musical development came when he discovered the fine sound of soul-music, and immersed himself full on in learning the intricate vocal phrasings of heroes such as Stevie Wonder and Ray Charles. What separates Mike from many of his peers is his natural “feel” for music, an ability to channel it with a ferocious effortlessness and authenticity that would normally be elusive for a ‘white boy’ from Canada.

If there’s anything else that separates Mike as a performer, it’s his ability to engage his audiences with his larger than life personality. “I really genuinely love ‘the hang’, that people at a show feel like they are at home with their friends,” Mike says. “I’m pretty easy going and I enjoy having a good time, so it’s important for me that the audience is sharing in that energy.”

With over 12 years of performing and engaging audiences full-time in a number of different original projects, cover bands, and playing guitar and singing back-up for other Canadian artists, Mike is now ready to record the songs he’s been toiling over for the last two years. “I have a lot to say and a lot to give – both lyrically and musically. This music is a part of who I am and is for anyone who wants to share it with me” he says. When it comes to his future Mike says, “It’s an honour and privilege to do what I do, and I just want to get better. I’ve learned so much from my mentors and peers, and I just want to keep going further – patiently and persistently.”

We’re glad that music chose Mike.
